About

Introduction

I am an undergraduate student in the Department of Physics at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. My passion lies in the field of condensed matter physics. During my undergraduate studies, I took the initiative to establish the Physics Student Club and assumed the role of External Vice President. In addition to my research pursuits, I also contribute to the academic community by conducting research and teaching CPHO (China Physics Olympiad) for Harbin Normal University High School and Shenzhen Senior High School on a part-time basis.

My research interests primarily revolve around the characterization and properties of topological materials. To further develop my expertise, I actively sought research opportunities in experimental physics within relevant research groups. These experiences have provided me with valuable hands-on training and a deeper understanding of the subject matter.Additionally, I have taken the initiative to delve into the field of computational materials science on my own. Through self-study, I have gained proficiency in this area and am currently conducting research in computational physics.

Overall, my undergraduate journey has been enriched by my involvement in research, leadership, and teaching roles. I am dedicated to pushing the boundaries of knowledge in condensed matter physics and inspiring the next generation of physicists through my research and teaching endeavors.
